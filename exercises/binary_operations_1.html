What is A_BIN \wedge B_BIN?

SOLUTION

\wedge is the symbol of logical conjunction, or the "and" operation.

"Anding" two bits gives 1 if they are both 1. Otherwise, it gives 0.

The first bit is A_BIN and the second bit is B_BIN. They are both 1. They are not both 1.

A_BIN \wedge B_BIN = SOLUTION_BIN

What is A_BIN \vee B_BIN?

SOLUTION

\vee is the symbol of logical disjunction, or the "or" operation.

"Oring" two bits gives 1 if either the first one, the second one, or both of them are 1, and 0 otherwise.

The first bit is A_BIN and the second bit is B_BIN. There is at least one 1 amongst them. They are both 0.

A_BIN \vee B_BIN = SOLUTION_BIN

A ^ B formatBinary( SOLUTION, 1 )

What is A_BIN \oplus B_BIN?

SOLUTION

\oplus is the symbol of exclusive disjunction, or the "XOR" operation.

The XOR of two bits is 1 if the bits are not equal, and 0 otherwise.

The first bit is A_BIN and the second bit is B_BIN. They are equal. They are not equal.

A_BIN \oplus B_BIN = SOLUTION_BIN

A ? 0 : 1 formatBinary( SOLUTION, 1 )

What is \sim A_BIN?

\sim A_BIN
SOLUTION

\sim is the symbol of binary negation.

Binary negation flips a bit, so a 0 turns to a 1 and a 1 turns to a 0.

\sim A_BIN = SOLUTION_BIN
